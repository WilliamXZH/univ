Distingushing Features of a Waldorf School Classroom

by Scott Dorwart and P.J. Long

There are more than 500 independent Waldorf schools in 32 countries.Through each one is unique,a visitor would observe common characteristic which distinguish the Waldorf approach to learning.Most notable among these would be how the curriculum directly responds to each phase of child development.

During the elementary years,it is the educator’s task to transform all that the child needs to know about the world into the language of the imagination—encouraging wonder,curiosty,reverence,and a love of learning.This principle lies at the heart of the most common features which distinguish Waldorf from other forms of education:

  1. All learning proceeds from a wholistic vision of the child
  2. Lessons derive from an arts-based,integrated curiculum.
  3. Expectations for learning are developmentally appropriate.
  4. The teacher remains with the class as the children grow.
  5. No graders are assigned to students’work.
  6. Behavior is managed through creative,positive discipline.
  7. The classroom provides a cooprative social environment.
Scott Dorwart is a carpenter and father of weo children who attend the Green Mountain Waldorf School. P.J. Long is a spychotherapist and mother of two.
